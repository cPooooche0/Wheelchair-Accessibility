What is Universal Design?

Definition?

Universal Design is defined by the overall design and composition incorporated to a built environment,product or service that can be accessible, understandable and usable across a wide range of people, no matter of their age or ability

This is covered by 7 principles

Principle 1:
Equitable Use

To create a design that is useful and marketable towards people of various abilities by:

  • Providing equal means for everyone
  • Avoiding segregating
  • Privacy,Security and safetly is available for everyone
  • Satisfying design for everyone

Principle 2:
Flexibility in Use

For a design to accommodate a wide range of individual preferences and abilities by:

  • Providing choices in methods of use
  • Accommodating right or left handed accessilbilty and use.
  • Placing adaptability to the user's pace
  • Providing adaptability to the user's pace

Principle 3:
Simple and Intuitive Use

The design's use is easy to understand, no matter the user's skills or intelligence by:

  • Taking out any excess complicated elements
  • Being consistent with user's expectations and desires
  • Accomodating for a wide vareity of literacy and language skills
  • Prioritise consistent information being arranged
  • Providing in effective feedback during and after a task is complete.

Principle 4:
Perceptible Information

The design communicates the neccessary information effectively, no matter the environment and user's sensory abilities by:

  • Using different selection of modes (such as oral or tactile) for the important information's presentation
  • Providing a suitable contrast between important information and its surroundings
  • Maximizing the legibility of important infomation
  • Providing clear communincations of instructions and directions.
  • Providing compatibility with a variety of techniques or devices by people with sensory limitations.

Principle 5:
Tolerance for Error

The design reduces hazards and the unfortunate consequences of accidental or unintended actions by:

  • Arranging elements to protection from hazards and errors
  • Providing warnings of any hazards and errors
  • Providing fail safe features
  • Discourage unconscious action in tasks that require major attention.

Principle 6:
Low Physical Effort

The design is used in a very efficent, comfortable and less tiring way by:

  • Allowing users to maintain a neutral body position
  • Allowing use of doable operating forces
  • Reducing repetitive actions
  • Providing reduced supportive physical effort

Principle 7:
Size and Space for Approach and Use

The design has approriate size and space provided for its approach, reach, manipulation and no matter the user's body's skills, size or position by:

  • Providing a clear line of visibilty towards important elements for any seated or standing users
  • Putting effort to reach all components and elements comfortabley for any seated or standing users
  • Accomodating variations in hand grip sizes
  • Provding suitable space for the use of assistive devices or personal assistance

What is Digital Accessibility

It is the creation of websites and many other technological outputs which are designed and developed in a way that people of all sorts of disabilites can use them with ease.

What is European Accessibility Act
